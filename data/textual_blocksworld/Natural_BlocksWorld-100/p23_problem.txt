In the Blocksworld game, you start with 15 blocks arranged as follows: Block 4 is sitting on the table, with Block 14 on top of it, and Block 8 on top of Block 14. Block 10 is directly on the table. Block 1 is also on the table, as well as Block 12 and Block 6. Block 7 is stacked on Block 6, with Block 5 on top of Block 7. Block 3 is on the table, with Block 13 on top of Block 3, Block 15 on Block 13, and Block 9 on top of Block 15. Lastly, Block 11 is on the table with Block 2 on top of it. You start with an empty arm and your goal is to rearrange the blocks into a new configuration: Block 13 should be on the table with Block 2 on top of it, Block 9 should be on the table, Block 1 should be on the table, and Block 11 should also be on the table. Block 7 needs to be on the table, with Block 12 on top of Block 7. Block 14 should be on the table with Block 5 on top of it, and Block 8 on top of Block 5. Block 10 should be on the table, and Block 15 should be on the table as well. Block 4 should be on the table with Block 6 on top of it, and Block 3 on top of Block 6.
